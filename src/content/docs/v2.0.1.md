# ReBirth RB-338 — Version 2.0.1

**Release date:** February 1999  
**Developer:** Propellerhead Software AB, Stockholm

## Overview

Version 2.0.1 is the **final commercial release** of ReBirth RB-338. It is a focused
maintenance release addressing the stability issues reported in 2.0, adding Mac OS 9
compatibility, and shipping a handful of quality-of-life improvements.

In 2005, Propellerhead Software released ReBirth RB-338 as **free abandonware**,
allowing the community to distribute and use it without restriction. The complete
installer, all official mod packs, and an offline manual were made available at
`www.rebirthmuseum.com` (now archived).

## New in 2.0.1

### Mac OS 9 Support

A fully recompiled Carbon-compatible build adds first-class Mac OS 9 support.
The PPC build now targets both OS 8.6 and OS 9.x with a single universal installer.

### Improved Audio Buffer Management

The audio engine now exposes a configurable **Buffer Size** setting (64, 128, 256, 512
samples) enabling lower-latency performance on fast hardware and stability on older systems.

### Pattern Export (Experimental)

An undocumented feature accessible via a keyboard shortcut exports raw step-sequencer
data as a plain-text CSV — useful for importing patterns into early DAWs.

## End of Commercial Life

Propellerhead shifted focus to Reason (released 2000) following the 2.0.1 launch.
ReBirth received no further commercial updates.

## Legacy & Preservation

The community has continued to maintain ReBirth compatibility under modern operating
systems through wrapper solutions:

- **Wine** (Linux/macOS): runs the Windows installer natively
- **DOSBox / VirtualBox**: full original experience on legacy OS images
- **This archive**: preserving songs, mods, and documentation for future researchers

The `.rbs` and `.rbm` binary formats remain fully documented by community reverse
engineering efforts and are the basis for the planned WebAssembly playback module in
this archive.
